## Emerging Markets \- Africa Market Research

* **African Gaming Industry**: The African video game market is rapidly expanding, with sales expected to surpass $1 billion by 2024  
  * Expansion is driven by a young population and increasing smartphone usage.  
* **Market Size and Growth Projections**  
  * **Revenue Projections**: The African gaming market is projected to reach approximately USD 4.28 billion by 2024, with an expected annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.17% from 2024 to 2027, leading to a market volume of USD 5.42 billion by 2027\.  
  * **User Base Expansion**: The number of gamers in Africa is anticipated to reach 212.7 million by 2027, reflecting a growing engagement with gaming.  
* **Key Growth Drivers**  
  * **Mobile Gaming Dominance**: The surge in mobile gaming is a primary growth driver, facilitated by increased smartphone adoption and more affordable internet access. In countries like Nigeria, mobile penetration reached 51% in 2024, significantly contributing to gaming industry revenues.  
  * **Youthful Demographics**: Africa’s young population drives gaming industry growth, fueling high demand for interactive entertainment and market expansion.  
* **Regional Highlights**  
  * **Nigeria’s Contribution**: Nigeria stands out as a significant contributor to Africa’s gaming market, with revenues of USD 46 million. The country’s gaming industry is fragmented but shows potential for consolidation as user behavior evolves.  
  * **South Africa’s Market**: South Africa’s gaming market is also notable, with local studios gaining international attention. For instance, the acquisition of South African studio 24 Bit Games by U.S. publisher Annapurna Interactive highlights the region’s growing prominence.  
* **Challenges and Opportunities**  
  * **Infrastructure Limitations**: Challenges such as inadequate infrastructure, including inconsistent electricity and limited internet access, hinder market growth. However, ongoing improvements in these areas present opportunities for expansion.  
  * **Local Content Development**: There is a growing demand for games with culturally relevant content. African studios like Kiro’o Games in Cameroon and Maliyo Games in Nigeria are creating games that resonate with local audiences, aiming to replicate the success of other African cultural exports like Nollywood and Afrobeats.  
* **Investment Trends**  
  * **Increased Investments**: The African gaming sector is attracting significant investments. For example, Sony’s investment in South African games publisher Carry1st underscores the growing interest of major industry players in the African market.
