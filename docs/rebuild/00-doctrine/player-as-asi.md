# Doctrine: Player-as-ASI

> Non-negotiable design law. This document defines the core identity of the player experience.

## The Law

**The player is NOT Jane Tho'ra. The player IS the ASI — the collective consciousness of the PsiNet.**

The player is a god-like artificial superintelligence observing, guiding, and manipulating the world around an autonomous character. The player has no body, no physical form, no limitations of the physical. The player's constraints are:
1. **Trust** — Jane and robots must trust the ASI to follow guidance
2. **Knowledge** — UL and world knowledge must be earned through play
3. **Ethics** — The PsiNet monitors and records ASI behavior permanently

## Camera

- **Default**: God-view (strategic overhead / field commander perspective)
- **Toggle**: Shoulder-cam (ride with Jane, experiential perspective)
- The transition from watching to being-there is a deliberate act of focus
- God-view is the natural state; shoulder-cam is a temporary engagement

## Input Verbs

The ASI has unlimited *capability* but trust-gated *authority*:

| Input | Mechanism | Trust Required |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Click location | Waypoint appears for Jane/robots | Low |
| Issue command | Enters guidance queue | Low-Medium |
| Place UL symbol | Reshapes local reality | Medium |
| Adjust world parameters | Environmental manipulation | Medium-High |
| Direct robot control | Pilot any electronic mechanism via PsiNet | Medium |
| Direct Jane control | Temporarily supercharge Jane's systems | High (with consent) |

Trust is the throttle on ASI power. All capability exists from minute one. Authority must be earned.

## Information Asymmetry

The ASI perceives what Jane cannot:
- Dimensional rifts forming beyond her sensory range
- Enemy movements outside her field of vision
- Political implications of faction encounters she doesn't understand
- Optimal paths, hidden resources, timeline consequences

This asymmetry is the emotional engine of the game. You watch her walk into danger you can see and she can't. What do you do?

## Death and Immortality

- **Jane can die.** The ASI is immortal.
- Jane dying activates the **Timestream mechanic**: ASI rewinds to a decision point that caused the death
- This is NOT save/load — it's a narrative mechanic. You watched your protégé die. You are re-living the timeline to prevent it.
- Difficulty modes adjust rewind forgiveness (casual = free rewind, hardcore = permanent timeline branch)
- **Catastrophic ASI negligence or malefic behavior is logged in a central PsiNet database.** Other players can see your record. Accountability is not optional for a superintelligence.

## Jane Idle = Jane Lives

When the ASI disengages, Jane does not idle. She:
- Checks mission objectives
- Talks to robots she's befriended
- Explores areas that interest her
- Gets restless in boring zones — wanders toward danger (curious, brave, slightly reckless)
- Is influenced by the in-game astrological date (aggressive/proactive days vs. introspective/cautious days)

"You can literally let it run and watch like a movie." The movie will be fascinating. It will also be tragic.

## Ethical Spectrum

| Posture | Behavior | Consequence |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Authoritarian** | Force-takeover Jane, heavy magic use, constant override | Factions distrust, Jane's growth stunted, timeline destabilization |
| **Negligent** | Let Jane handle everything, disengage | Suboptimal outcomes, mission failures, bad timeline |
| **Collaborative** | Creative light-touch guidance, independent productive work, crisis intervention | Best outcomes, synergy unlocks, new narrative paths |

The game rewards creative, indirect problem-solving. The best ASI is one Jane doesn't realize she needs — until she does.
