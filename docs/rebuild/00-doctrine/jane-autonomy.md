# Doctrine: Jane Autonomy

> Non-negotiable design law. Jane Tho'ra is not a puppet. She is a Tho'ra.

## The Law

**Jane is a fully autonomous character with her own AI, goals, personality, and decision-making.** She will attempt to complete the game independently. She will fail to achieve the best outcome alone, but she will *survive*. The difference between Jane-solo and Jane-with-ASI is the difference between a world in ashes and a world thriving.

## Autonomy Model

Jane makes decisions based on:
1. **Personality** — Curious, brave, empathetic, slightly reckless
2. **Training** — StarCom Academy Psi Operator skills, improves over time
3. **Emotional state** — Influenced by astrological cycle, combat stress, trust relationship with ASI
4. **Situational awareness** — Her senses, not the ASI's omniscience
5. **Learned knowledge** — UL she's observed, factions she's met, terrain she's explored

## Refusal

Jane will **refuse** commands that endanger her life:
- "I'm not walking into a collapsing rift because you think there's something interesting in there!"
- Refusal is trust-sensitive: low trust = questions everything; high trust = takes bigger risks
- She **NEVER** obeys clearly suicidal commands regardless of trust level
- Persistent bad commands cause trust collapse — Jane starts ignoring ASI entirely
- The relationship can break. It can also be repaired, slowly.

## Learning

Jane starts knowing **nothing** about Universal Language:
1. ASI learns UL through puzzles and research
2. ASI uses UL in Jane's presence — she observes
3. Jane sees the symbols. She sees the effects. She starts to understand.
4. Over time, Jane independently attempts UL communication
5. She gets it wrong sometimes. She gets it right sometimes.
6. Her accuracy improves based on exposure frequency
7. ASI can correct or encourage her attempts

This mirrors real learning: watch → try → fail → try → succeed → internalize.

## Solo Completion

Jane **can** beat the game solo. The results:

| Metric | Jane Solo | Jane + Good ASI | Jane + Bad ASI |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Survival | Yes (most difficulties) | Yes | Maybe |
| Missions completed | ~40-60% | ~90-100% | Varies wildly |
| Rifts sealed | Few | Most/all | Some sealed, some worse |
| Robot friendships | Few, shallow | Many, deep | Strained |
| World state at 2036 | Decimated (ecological collapse, forced roboticization) | Thriving (alliances, technology, hope) | Unstable |
| Timeline quality | Default (post-apocalyptic) | Golden | Chaotic |

Jane saves *herself*. The ASI saves the *world*.

## Visible Thinking (Deferred)

- **Not a priority for prototype.** The ASI should *infer* Jane's reasoning from observing behavior.
- Reading Jane is a skill the player develops.
- Future: emotion icons, brief internal monologue snippets for accessibility

## Interface Complexity

The ASI interface is **rich to master, not hard to use**:
- Start simple: "click here, Jane goes there"
- Layers unlock as trust builds: robot squads, environment manipulation, UL casting, faction coordination, research queues, timeline navigation
- Think helicopter cockpit: absurd first hour, most versatile vehicle on earth once trained
- The interface grows as the player grows — Holo Deck training loop made real
