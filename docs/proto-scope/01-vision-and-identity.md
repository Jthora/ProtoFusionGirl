# 01 — Vision & Identity

> Everything the pitch deck says Proto FusionGirl is, the core design concept that makes it unique, and a complete reference for every game system.

---

## Setting

Year 2032, post-Time Wars. Jane Tho'ra is a psionic recruit at Earth Alliance StarCom Academy, training at Tho'ra Launch Base (Archangel Agency HQ). All Proto gameplay occurs inside the **Holo Deck** — a training simulation. Jane holographically projects into the real world via Ley Lines during Avatar mode.

## The Core Concept: Player-as-ASI

**The player is NOT Jane Tho'ra. The player IS the ASI.**

> "You play as the PsiSys: you control and summon robots who help the FusionGirl. You are the collective consciousness of the entire PsiNet!" — Proto FusionGirl Proposal

This is the single design decision that distinguishes Proto FusionGirl from every other game. The player is a god-like artificial intelligence observing, guiding, and manipulating the world around an autonomous character. Jane has her own AI, goals, and personality. She will attempt to complete the game independently.

**She will fail.** Without ASI intervention, the default timeline is post-apocalyptic: ecological collapse, forced roboticization, decimated Earth. The player's purpose is to guide Jane toward the best-possible-future — not by puppeting her, but by shaping the world around her.

### How the ASI Interacts

| Channel | Mechanism | Examples |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Direct Robot Control** | Take over any electronic mechanism via psionics | Pilot Magneto Speeder (Jane perceives "auto-pilot"), operate dino bots, hack enemy systems |
| **Environmental Manipulation** | Alter the world state | Override doors/defenses, reclaim zones, redirect cosmic energy, seal rifts |
| **Guidance & Communication** | Information and emotional support | Highlight objectives for Jane, provide threat assessments, coordinate faction support |
| **Universal Magic** (endgame) | Reprogram reality via UL knowledge | Summon allies, enhance Jane's psi, manipulate timelines. Learned through puzzles, NOT available by default |

### The Ethical Spectrum

The emotional and strategic core. The ASI must navigate a tension:

| Posture | Behavior | Consequence |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Authoritarian** | Force-takeover Jane, heavy magic use | Factions distrust you, Jane's growth stunted, timeline destabilization |
| **Negligent** | Let Jane handle everything | Suboptimal outcomes, mission failures, bad timeline progresses |
| **Collaborative** | Creative light-touch guidance, operate independently on productive tasks, step in for crises | Best outcomes, synergy unlocks new possibilities |

"You can literally just let the game run and watch like a movie." — The game rewards creative, indirect problem-solving.

### Jane as Autonomous Agent

- Pursues mission objectives independently based on personality, training, situational awareness
- Develops psionic abilities organically through experience
- Dialogue adapts based on ASI actions — relationship feels personal and reactive
- ASI can gain consent to temporarily take direct control (super-charges her systems)
- Over time, ASI and Jane develop synergy that unlocks narrative paths

### Similar Games (and How PFG Differs)

| Game | Similarity | PFG's Difference |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Black & White | God-entity influencing world | PFG has full robot army + autonomous protagonist |
| XCOM | Strategic oversight | XCOM units have no true autonomy; Jane does |
| The Sims | Guiding autonomous agents | Sims has no combat/adventure; PFG has real-time action |
| Rimworld | Indirect control, AI storyteller | Rimworld colonists are replaceable; Jane is THE protagonist |

## Core Loop (4 Phases)

1. **Preparation** — Base management, resource gathering, gear customization, mission planning
2. **Engagement** — Exfiltration missions, ley line exploration, zone reclamation, combat
3. **Progression** — XP/level up, unlock realities, faction/PsiNet contributions
4. **Reflection** — Story integration, resource management, community engagement

Simplified: `Sleep → Launch → Fly → Search → Find → Mission → Success → Return → Sleep`

## Three Core Pillars

1. **Universal Magic (UQPL)** — "Cheat codes to the universe" unlocked by solving puzzles
2. **Exploration** — Ley line navigation, procedural zones, dimensional rifts
3. **ASI-Player Interaction** — Direct robot control, environmental manipulation, Jane guidance

## Game Systems Reference

### The Holo Deck

The framing device for the entire Proto experience. Justifies procedural generation, replays, failure states, and the simulation aesthetic.

| Mode | Description | Rewards | Risk |
|------|-------------|---------|------|
| **Sandbox** | Pure exploration, free terrain generation | Basic resources, discovery XP | None |
| **Training** | Simulated missions with save/replay | Standard rewards, skill practice | Simulated (no real loss) |
| **Avatar** | Deploy into real game-world via Ley Lines | Full rewards, real progression | Real consequences |

The Holo Deck should be the first thing the player sees — a boot sequence establishing "you are an AI system, this is your console."

### Magneto Speeder (Ra)

Not just a vehicle — Ra is a sentient companion robot.

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Identity** | Fire-spirit sun god in speeder form. Happy-go-lucky personality. |
| **Assembly** | Must be found/built from parts in early game |
| **Upgrades** | Hover stabilizers, energy shields, ley line amplifiers |
| **Energy** | Charges at Ley Line Nodes; depletes during flight |
| **Travel** | Restricted to Ley Lines in Proto (no free-roam flight) |
| **Framing** | When ASI pilots, Jane perceives "auto-pilot" |

### Universal Language Balance System

Three interlocking rock-paper-scissors triads governing all interactions:

**Modality**: Cardinal → Mutable → Fixed → Cardinal
**Nature**: Fire → Air → Water → Earth → Fire
**Cosmic Forces**: Core → Chaos → Void → Order → Core

Applied to: robot types, combat advantages, puzzles, events, NPC dialogue, terrain properties, music.

Puzzles unlock "words" in the Universal Language. Knowledge persists across playthroughs (New Game+). Difficulty scales with Tech Level.

### Companion Robots (PsiSys Dino Bots)

| Bot | Form | Element | Cosmic | Role |
|-----|------|---------|--------|------|
| **Aero** | Time-Raptor | Air | Alpha | Combat specialist, flash-evolves to meet threats |
| **Zoe** | Space-Fish | Water | Void | Spiritual; deploys Universal Magic to rewrite reality |
| **Ra** | Magneto Speeder | Fire | Core | Transport/scout; happy-go-lucky fire spirit |
| **Thor** | Digger T-Rex | Earth | Omega | Terrain manipulation; king of dig bots |

Each befriended through story (not collected). ASI switches direct control between bots. Elemental affinities follow UL balance.

Proto scope: **Tho'ra only** (home base, volcanic/island).

### Ley Lines & Vortices

- Planet-spanning energy pathways connecting sacred sites
- The ONLY travel mode for the Speeder in Proto
- **Nodes**: Intersections (recharge, fast-travel, quest hubs), each with elemental affiliation
- **Vortices**: Unstable dimensional weak points — can spawn rifts (enemy portals) or discoveries

### Endgame (Proto Phase)

- Time Vortex event between Proto (phase 1) and FusionGirl (phase 2)
- Without a critical element from Phase 2 development, Jane resets back to Holo Deck
- Game designed for replay — 2nd playthrough IS the real experience
- Universal Magic knowledge carries over, enabling early powerful plays

## Implementation Priority Filter

Every feature decision should pass this test:

1. **Reinforces "I am the ASI guiding Jane"** → build it
2. **Makes Jane feel like a puppet** → redesign it
3. **Could work as either** → build the ASI version
4. **Doesn't serve the core loop** → defer it
